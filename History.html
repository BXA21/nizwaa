Timeline of Nizwa

6th century AD

Nizwa was founded by the Imri dynasty.

16th century AD

Nizwa became the capital of Oman under the Ya'aruba dynasty.

1670 AD

The Nizwa Fort was built by Imam Sultan bin Saif Al Ya'rubi.

1954 AD

The first school in Nizwa was established by the Ministry of Education.

1970 AD

Sultan Qaboos bin Said overthrew his father and became the Sultan of Oman, leading to modernization and development of Nizwa.

1985 AD

The Nizwa Souq was renovated and restored to its traditional architecture and style.

1993 AD

The Nizwa Friday cattle market was relocated to a new modern facility outside the city center.

2021 AD

Nizwa was designated as the Capital of Islamic Culture for the Arab region by the Islamic Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (ISESCO).
